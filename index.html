Monitoring Health Risks

BMI is often used as an indicator of potential
health risks associated with weight.
Higher BMI values are generally associated with an increased
risk of various health conditions like heart disease,
type 2 diabetes, certain cancers, and musculoskeletal disorders.

Setting Health Goals

If your BMI falls outside the normal range, knowing your BMI
can serve as a starting point to set health goals.
It can help you establish realistic targets for weight management,
whether it's aiming to reach a healthier weight, maintain your
current weight, or gain weight in the case of being underweight

Tracking Progress

Monitoring changes in your BMI over time can help you track your progress
towards achieving your health goals. By regularly measuring and recording your BMI,
you can assess the effectiveness of your lifestyle choices,
such as diet and exercise, and make necessary adjustments as needed.
